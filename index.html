Butt-ons Simple: in design, to remember.

[ Documentation ] [ Examples ] [ Contribute ] [ Issues ] [ @zafarali ]

We've all wanted some simple buttons that complement our site look good. Buttons that fit in with the new flat revolution. We also want to easily remember how to class our buttons. Here, I present to you a Order-inpependant, forgiving and chained way of styling buttons.

Regular ol' buttons .butt-on

Simple, Classy and Elegant, this narrow button should be the basis of all your button needs

Anchors 
<button class="butt-on">Buttons</button>
<a href="#regular" class="butt-on">Anchors</a>
<input type="submit" class="butt-on" value="Inputs?">

Inheritance .butt-on

All buttons will inherit their parents' font.

<button class="butt-on">Click Me!</button>

Modifiers .-round, .-transp, .-border, .-over

You can use the above prefixes to modify your buttons. And since Butt-on is forgiving, you can choose between .transparent or .transp, .round or .rounded

or 
<button class="butt-on-transparent">Transp</button>
<button class="butt-on-transp">Transparent?</button>
Rounded 
<a class="butt-on-rounded">Rounded</a>

You can combine them...

Rounded and Transparent 
<a class="butt-on-rounded-transp">Rounded and Transparent</a>

In any order!

<a class="butt-on-transp-round">Rounded and Transparent</a>

We can override our button color,so that it has the default color but on hover is of a different color!

<button class="butt-on-red-over">Red, Override</button>

We can force a colored button to have a border.

<button class="butt-on-red-border">Red, Border</button>

Size Control .-wide, -high, -ultrawide, -ultrahigh

Sometimes we want to make our buttons wider or taller. This is easy, we just need to specify what we need.

I'm high 
<button class="butt-on-wide">I'm wide</button>
<a class="butt-on-high">I'm high</a>

Ofcourse we can combine the two (in any order!)

I'm (ultra) high and (ultra) wide 
<a class="butt-on-ultrahigh-ultrawide">I'm (ultra) high and (ultra) wide</a>

Colors .-red, .-turq, .-green, .-blue, .-purple, .-yellow, .-orange, .-offwhite, .-grey, .-gray

All buttons need to be versatile in their colors! No longer do we need to remember btn-danger or btn-warn, or was it btn-red? Just prefix, in any order, with any combination of modifiers or size controllers.

Red Buttons Purple Buttons Blue Buttons Green Buttons Yellow Buttons Turqoise Buttons Orange Buttons Offwhite Buttons Gray Buttons Grey Buttons

The synax is forgiving so .-turq is the same as .-turquoise and you can even spell Gray/Grey however you want.
